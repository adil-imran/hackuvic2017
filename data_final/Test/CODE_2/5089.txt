Home | News | General | Tell Buhari the truth or this country will be destroyed – Top 10 quotes of the week

As the weeks run by, Nigeria continues to thrive on what many have called the fragile peace. There is a recession besetting the progress of the economy, there is a war against corruption and there is agitation for a divided country.

Below are 10 quotable quotes that speaks volume of what Nigeria as a nation passed through in the passing week.

1.”When a lie is repeated over and over, it begins to sound like the truth”

Chief Sunny Onuesoke, a chieftain of the Peoples Democratic Party (PDP) in Delta state, saying that Nigeria would have been a better country without President Muhammadu Buhari.

2. “Boko Haram is a menace that does nobody any good in Nigeria”

Chief Olusegun Obasanjo applauding President Muhammadu Buhari for the success recorded in the fight against Boko Haram and blamed the growth of the insurgency on the Goodluck Jonathan administration

3. “Politicians across the nation would engage in alignments and re-alignments for the creation of two new political parties”

Former president of Nigeria, Chief Olusegun Obasanjo, revealing that two new political parties would be established before the end of 2017.

4. “As a combat politician, he fought to take over the Presidential Villa despite being told he would never see it”

President Muhammadu Buhari being eulogised by eminent personalities who graced the occasion of the public presentation of a book titled Muhammadu Buhari: The Challenges of Leadership in Nigeria.

5. “ACF should support the government where it is right and should also talk where the government goes wrong”

Nigerian activist-turned senator, Shehu Sani warning leaders in the north and members of the Arewa Consultative Forum against supporting President Muhammadu Buhari blindly.

6. “Jesus would welcome and embrace gays, transsexuals”

Pope Francis has recently calling for transsexual and homosexual people to be accepted and respected by the Catholic Church.

7. “Every person every person shall be entitled to freedom of expression including freedom to hold opinions and to receive and impact ideas and information without interference”

Julie Ward making a plea to President Muhammadu Buhari, for the quick release of Mazi Nnamdi Kanu.

8. “Nigerian governors are so lazy they cannot handle their own families”

Alhaji Yerima Shettima, the president of the Arewa Youth Consultative Forum (AYCF). In this interview with Eromosele Ebhomele, he dissects the Muhammadu Buhari government

9. “Nigeria is an enclave where nothing works and can never work because the system has been so rigged and programmed for a catastrophic failure”

The Indigenous People of Biafra (IPOB) saying that Nigerian government has no option but to face the law at the Economic Community of West African States Court of Justice.

10. “These claims are false and poorly sourced to present a picture of vendetta; and they should be disregarded by well-meaning members of the public”

President Muhammadu Buhari denying the freezing the accounts of former president Goodluck Jonathan as was claimed by some Bayelsa youths in a recent protest.
