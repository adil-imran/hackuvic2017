Guatemala has arrested more than a dozen ex-army officers, including a former chief of staff, over massacres at the height of the country’s 36-year civil war.

Among their alleged crimes are the “disappearances” of at least 558 people from indigenous Mayan communities in the north of the country between 1981 and 1988.

Those detained included the former chief of staff Manuel Benedicto Lucas Garcia, 83, whose brother ruled the country from 1978 to 1982. A former interior minister was also arrested. Prosecutors have called for the former dictator, Efraín Ríos Montt, to face charges of genocide.
